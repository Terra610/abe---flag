What CCRI Does

CCRI evaluates how lenders, dealers, and underwriters use data when deciding who gets access to credit and vehicles. It flags when:

  • DMV or DOT data is used for non–DOT-regulated citizens.
  • License status or traffic enforcement is treated as a gatekeeper for basic economic mobility.
  • State-created barriers are silently driving private credit decisions.

CCRI never scores people. It scores the systems and rules that stand between people and lawful access to credit, transportation, and work.

Inputs

CCRI reads scenarios defined in ccri/inputs.json. Each scenario represents a specific lender, dealership, or underwriting policy set and describes:

  • Which data sources are used (credit bureaus, DMV, DOT, background vendors).
  • Whether license status is required to apply, test drive, or fund a loan.
  • How many applicants are denied based on DMV or license-related criteria.
  • Whether there is any appeal or human review path.

The engine then calls the shared A.B.E. constitutional layer to determine where those practices diverge from federal law and the Constitution.

Outputs & Linkage

CCRI produces scores and metrics that are consumed by other A.B.E. modules:

  • CIRI: rights and enforcement divergence tied to credit and mobility barriers.
  • CFF: financial exposure where unlawful data is embedded in underwriting models.
  • CIBS: how many households and how much GDP can be unlocked by correcting the rules.
  • AFFE: long-term stability, default reductions, and community-level recovery.

Together, these modules show how correcting credit and lending practices can restore both constitutional fidelity and economic opportunity.
