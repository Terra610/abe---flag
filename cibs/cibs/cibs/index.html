CIBS — Community Integrity Budget System

CIBS turns the dollar value recovered by lawful correction (the number produced by CIRI) into a transparent reinvestment budget that the public can audit. This is not charity. This is repair.

1. Start with the recovery pool

CIRI gives us a number like: $5,201,525 for Example County 2025. We call that the available_pool. 

available_pool = 5,201,525

2. Allocate by category

We apply policy percentages from budget_template.csv to decide where that money goes: 

category_amount = available_pool × percent_of_pool

Example (25% to housing):

5,201,525 × 0.25 = $1,300,381.25  → Community Housing Support

Categories are defined in plain language: housing stability, youth & aftercare, veterans/emergency relief, local small business recovery, digital access, legal defense, transparency dashboard, and compliance.

3. Schedule it

Every category gets mapped to a time schedule (monthly or quarterly) and a named steward. That schedule is published. The public can ask: “Did that money actually get there?”

4. Why this matters

CIBS makes it impossible for a jurisdiction to say “we fixed the problem” and then bury the money. Every dollar has a destination, timeline, and purpose.
