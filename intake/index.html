Intake — Document + OCR Bridge

Turn a ticket, CPS letter, loan contract, or memo into structured, hash-locked data that CIRI, CDA, CFF, and CCRI can use — without your document ever leaving your device.

1. Upload a document

Start with what you actually have: a photo of a ticket, a scanned PDF, or a text file. This runs entirely in your browser. No uploads. No accounts. No tracking.


No file selected.

OCR (machine reading) runs for images (photos/scans). Text files are read directly. PDF support depends on your browser’s ability to expose text — images-only PDFs may behave like images.

2. Tell Intake what this is

This doesn’t classify you. It tells the engine which lenses to use downstream.


Which parts of A.B.E. should this feed?

3. Extracted text (editable)

This is what the engine sees. You can clean it up before creating your intake artifact.

Tip: If names, addresses, or case numbers are sensitive, you can replace them with your own IDs before generating the artifact. The math doesn’t care about labels.

4. Generate Intake Artifact

This builds a JSON file that captures: file hash, document type, normalized text, and your selected modules. You can attach it to motions, audits, or research.

The artifact follows intake/schema.json: it includes original_file_hash, doc_type, text_normalized, targets, and hashes of any generated downstream files.
